# Tighten instance-service @service design after architect review

The instance-`@service` parity fix shipped at `4867dc0` ("Support instance-method @service classes via (DSLContext) constructor") restored the legacy generator's behaviour: a developer `@service` method on an instance class with a `public ServiceName(DSLContext)` constructor compiles cleanly again, and so do downstream consumers (Sikt opptak-subgraph and similar) that hand-rolled this pattern. The bug fix itself is sound — `serviceCallTarget` at `TypeFetcherGenerator.java:1251-1255` is the right call-site fork, and the `service-catalog-instance-service-holder-shape` `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` / `@DependsOnClassifierCheck` pair makes the producer/consumer contract auditable. But the patch landed without a Spec, and a post-hoc principles-architect review surfaced four model-shape tensions, two test-shape tensions, and one documentation tension. This item ships the cleanup.

The architectural smell at the root: the static-vs-instance axis is carried as a `default true` boolean on `MethodRef`, which papers over three distinct populations — a classification axis on `@service` (`true | false`, both reachable), a structural invariant on `@tableMethod` / `@externalField` (must be static; `@externalField` actively enforces this, `@tableMethod` does not but should), and a structurally-static-by-construction case on `ConditionFilter` (a `@condition` resolves to `static Condition method(Table<?>, ...)`; the variant is always-static by contract, never reads as instance). The fix is to make the static-vs-instance axis visible only on the variant where it can vary (`@service`), and unreachable on the variants where it cannot. That structural unreachability is the type-system upgrade the boolean default cannot deliver.

A first-pass spec defended `default new CallShape.Static()` on the interface as that upgrade. Architect review (in-session, before hand-off) pushed back: a default-on-interface relocates the same papering-over from `default true` to `default new Static()` — the populations stay the same, the smell moves. The principle (`rewrite-design-principles.adoc:39-43`, *narrow component types over broad interfaces*) reads in favour of splitting the producer-level concrete type so that only `@service` carries `CallShape` and the static-only producers (`@tableMethod`, `@externalField`, enum-mapping wrapping a `@tableMethod`) cannot reach `CallShape.InstanceWithDslHolder` at all. This revised plan adopts that reading. The Out-of-scope #4 carve in the first pass ("lifting `MethodRef` itself to a sealed hierarchy") is dropped: the lift IS the correct shape, not minimum-change for its own sake.

## Implementation

### Phase A — Split `MethodRef.Basic` into `Service` / `StaticOnly`; lift static-vs-instance onto a sealed `CallShape` carried only by `Service` (§1, §6)

**Model (`graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/model/MethodRef.java`).**

- Seal `MethodRef`. Today the interface is open; the variant population is in fact closed (one `Basic` record + the `ConditionFilter` direct implementor). Lift to `sealed interface MethodRef permits Service, StaticOnly, ConditionFilter`. The `ConditionFilter` permit lives in its own file unchanged structurally (only the `permits` clause arrives).
- Replace the single `Basic` record with two record permits:
  - `Service` carries `(className, methodName, returnType, params, declaredExceptions, callShape)`. This is the only `MethodRef` variant whose call shape can vary; `reflectServiceMethod` is the only producer.
  - `StaticOnly` carries `(className, methodName, returnType, params, declaredExceptions)`. No `callShape` accessor — static-by-construction is the variant identity. Producers: `reflectTableMethod`, `reflectExternalField`, and `EnumMappingResolver` when wrapping a `StaticOnly` upstream (see Phase B for the variant-preserving switch).
- Introduce `sealed interface CallShape permits Static, InstanceWithDslHolder` as a nested type on `Service`. `Static` carries `boolean needsDslLocal` pre-resolved at classify time (any `ParamSource.DslContext` in the param list). `InstanceWithDslHolder` has no fields — `needsDsl` is always true on this arm by variant identity (the holder ctor needs the local regardless of the param list). Both consumers (`buildServiceFetcherCommon`, `buildServiceRowsMethod`) read `needsDsl` via a single accessor `method.callShape()`-paired pattern (see consumer bullet below); no consumer recomputes the param-side disjunction.
- Drop `MethodRef.isStatic()` (the `default true` accessor at `MethodRef.java:52`) entirely. The static-vs-instance distinction now lives in two places: variant identity (`StaticOnly` vs `Service`), and `CallShape` arm identity within `Service`. There is no flat boolean accessor to (mis-)default.
- Drop the 4-arg compat constructor at `MethodRef.java:172-174` and the 5-arg compat constructor at `:160-163` along with the `Basic` record itself. `Service` and `StaticOnly` each have one canonical constructor; there are no defaults left to silently miscarry.
- Update `MethodRef.java:32-46` javadoc: drop the "may produce false" prose on `isStatic`; replace with one paragraph mapping each permit to its producer set and its emit shape (`Service.callShape()` decides emit form on the `serviceCallTarget` path; `StaticOnly` rides the static call-target on the `@tableMethod` / `@externalField` paths; `ConditionFilter` rides the `@condition` evaluator path).

**Catalog (`graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/ServiceCatalog.java`).**

- `reflectServiceMethod` at `:194-199` keeps the `Modifier.isStatic` read but produces a `CallShape`: `new CallShape.Static(needsDslLocal)` for static methods (where `needsDslLocal = method.params().stream().anyMatch(p -> p.source() instanceof ParamSource.DslContext)`), or `new CallShape.InstanceWithDslHolder()` for instance methods that pass `checkServiceInstanceHolderShape`. The `Basic` constructor call at `:274-275` becomes a `Service` constructor with the `CallShape` threaded through.
- The `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` description at `:157-162` updates: "the `InstanceWithDslHolder` arm rejects abstract / interface / no-`(DSLContext)`-ctor holders…"; the consumer-side `reliesOn` strings update in lockstep (see §6 dedup below).
- `reflectTableMethod` at `:393-473` is changed in observable behaviour: see Phase B for the new positive `Modifier.isStatic` rejection arm and the `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` declaration. The producer threads `StaticOnly` (no compat-constructor default) so the static-by-construction guarantee is structural.

**Consumer (`graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/generators/TypeFetcherGenerator.java`).**

- `serviceCallTarget` at `:1251-1255` takes a `MethodRef.Service` (narrowed input — the bare `MethodRef` parameter goes away because the call site can only fire on the service path). The body switches exhaustively on `CallShape`: `$T` for `Static`, `new $T(dsl)` for `InstanceWithDslHolder`. A future third arm (e.g. a `ServiceHolderFactory` extension point — see "Out of scope") becomes a compile error at this site, not a silent fall-through.
- `buildServiceFetcherCommon` at `:1195-1197` and `buildServiceRowsMethod` at `:2447-2449` drop the `boolean needsDsl = !method.isStatic() || any-DslContext-param` recomputation entirely. Both sites read `needsDsl` from the `CallShape` arm via `method.callShape()`-paired pattern matching (`Static s -> s.needsDslLocal()` / `InstanceWithDslHolder _ -> true`), single source of truth. The disjunction is computed once at classify time inside `reflectServiceMethod`, not at every call site. The `needsDslLocalFor` helper proposed in the first pass is dropped — the per-arm accessor on `CallShape` is the cleaner shape.
- `EnumMappingResolver.enrichArgExtractions` at `EnumMappingResolver.java:201-222` becomes variant-preserving via a sealed switch on the upstream `MethodRef`: `Service s -> new Service(..., s.callShape())` (preserve the call shape end-to-end), `StaticOnly _ -> new StaticOnly(...)`, `ConditionFilter -> throw IllegalStateException("enum mapping is not applied to @condition refs")` (the call-site contract; `@condition` and enum-arg-mapping don't compose). The `method.isStatic()` read at `:221` is gone with the boolean accessor.

**§6 dedup of the duplicated `reliesOn` prose** lands in this phase. `TypeFetcherGenerator.java:1175-1181` (the `buildServiceFetcherCommon` consumer) keeps the full prose; the rows-method consumer at `:2419-2423` shortens to `"Same guarantee as buildServiceFetcherCommon's static-vs-instance fork."` so the rewrite-once-when-it-changes site is unambiguous.

### Phase B — Add the positive `@tableMethod` static-modifier check; drop compat constructors as a side effect of the variant split (§2, was Out-of-scope #3)

Two sub-changes that share a commit because they're entwined: the variant split kills the compat constructors structurally (no shared `Basic` to default), and the new `StaticOnly`-only path for `@tableMethod` introduces a load-bearing emitter assumption ("the reflected method actually IS static") that needs a producer-side check landing in the same commit per *Classifier guarantees shape emitter assumptions* (`rewrite-design-principles.adoc:107-122`).

- **Compat constructors gone.** With `Basic` split into `Service` and `StaticOnly`, the 4-arg and 5-arg compat constructors at `MethodRef.java:160-163` and `:172-174` simply don't exist on either successor record. Each successor has one canonical constructor with no defaults. Test fixtures across `MappingsConstantNameDedupTest`, `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest` (~22 sites), `ErrorMappingsClassGeneratorTest`, `ServiceFieldValidationTest`, `TableFieldValidationTest`, `QueryServiceRecordFieldValidationTest`, `MutationServiceTableFieldValidationTest`, `ComputedFieldValidationTest`, `RecordTableFieldValidationTest`, `MutationServiceRecordFieldValidationTest`, `TableMethodFieldValidationTest` migrate via two factories on `TestFixtures` mirroring R81's `tableRef(...)` factory pattern: `TestFixtures.staticOnlyMethodRef(...)` and `TestFixtures.serviceMethodRef(..., CallShape)`. The factories' existence becomes a sentinel that future tests can't accidentally pick the wrong variant by reaching for a bare ctor with a forgotten boolean.
- **Positive `@tableMethod` static check.** `reflectTableMethod` at `ServiceCatalog.java:393-473` adds an early rejection arm modelled on the existing `@externalField` template at `:513`. Sketch: after the parameter loop, before the `Basic` instantiation at `:466-468`, check `if (!java.lang.reflect.Modifier.isStatic(javaMethod.getModifiers())) return Rejection.structural("method '" + methodName + "' in class '" + className + "' must be declared 'static' for @tableMethod — instance @tableMethod methods are not supported; the call site emits 'ClassName.method(table, ...)' which requires a static method");`. The `StaticOnly` constructor call follows.
- **New `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` key.** Add `service-catalog-tablemethod-must-be-static` on `reflectTableMethod`, paired with `@DependsOnClassifierCheck(key = "service-catalog-tablemethod-must-be-static", reliesOn = "Emits 'ClassName.method(table, ...)' static-call shape unconditionally for @tableMethod refs; the catalog rejects instance @tableMethod methods at classify time so the emitter doesn't have to fork on call shape.")` on whichever `TypeFetcherGenerator` site emits the `@tableMethod` static-call shape. (The existing `service-catalog-strict-tablemethod-return` key carries the return-type guarantee; this is a sibling key for the static-modifier guarantee.) The annotation pair plus `LoadBearingGuaranteeAuditTest` makes the producer/consumer link auditable in the same way the strict-return pair is.
- **Validator-tier coverage** for the new rejection arm lands as a focused test in `TableMethodFieldValidationTest` (sibling of Phase C's service-side test): a fixture class with an instance `@tableMethod` is wired into a real schema, run through builder + validator, and the assertion checks for one `ValidationError` whose message names "must be declared 'static'". Validator mirrors classifier; the new producer doesn't escape `:103-105`.
- **Three production call sites in `ServiceCatalog` migrate.** `:274-275` (`reflectServiceMethod`) produces `Service` with the chosen `CallShape`; `:466-468` (`reflectTableMethod`) produces `StaticOnly`; `:551` (`reflectExternalField`) produces `StaticOnly` (the existing positive `Modifier.isStatic` check at `:513` already guarantees the static-by-construction shape — no new rejection needed for `@externalField`).

### Phase C — Validator-tier coverage for the instance-holder rejection (§4)

The `service-catalog-instance-service-holder-shape` `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` rejects abstract / interface / no-`(DSLContext)`-ctor holders structurally; `UnclassifiedField` carries the rejection through to `GraphitronSchemaValidator.validateUnclassifiedField` at `:844`. Today the only test that exercises the rejection is `ServiceCatalogTest.reflectServiceMethod_instanceMethodWithoutDslContextCtor_rejectedWithActionableMessage` — a unit-tier classifier test, not a validator-tier reachability test.

- Add a focused validator-tier test `ServiceFieldValidationTest.instanceServiceHolderShape_noCtor_validatorReportsAuthorError` (or sibling). It builds a real schema using `TestInstanceServiceStubNoCtor` as the `@service` class, runs the schema through `GraphitronSchemaBuilder` + `GraphitronSchemaValidator`, and asserts a `ValidationError` whose message names both options ("make the method static, or add the `(DSLContext)` constructor") and quotes the field's qualified name. This proves the rejection arm is reachable from the validator's own dispatch surface, matching the principle "validator mirrors classifier invariants" at `rewrite-design-principles.adoc:103-105`.
- No producer-side change. The `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` annotation already captures the contract; the new test gives it independent reachability evidence.

### Phase D — Replace body-string assertions with structural / pipeline / compilation tier coverage (§3)

The two new tests at `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest.java:1004-1007` (`mutationServiceRecordField_instanceMethod_emitsNewServiceWithDsl`) and `:1025-1028` (`mutationServiceRecordField_staticMethod_keepsStaticCallShape`) assert literal `code().toString()` substrings — `"new com.example.Service(dsl).doThing("`, `"com.example.Service.doThing("`, `"DSLContext dsl"`. Banned per `rewrite-design-principles.adoc:128` ("Code-string assertions on generated method bodies are banned at every tier; they test implementation, not behaviour, and break on every refactor").

- Retire both tests. Replace with a focused structural test `MethodRefCallShapeTest` at `graphitron/src/test/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/model/`. The test takes the load-bearing role for arm-routing regressions per *Pipeline tests are the primary behavioural tier* (`rewrite-design-principles.adoc:124-128`): a `CallShape.Static` produces the bare-class call-target shape via `serviceCallTarget` (return value is the `$T` form of the service class); an `InstanceWithDslHolder` `CallShape` produces the `new $T(dsl)` form; the sealed switch covers both and a hypothetical third arm would be a compile error. Assert via `CodeBlock` structural equality (using `equals` on the rendered `CodeBlock`, not `toString`-substring). This is the assertion that fires *first* on a regression where the emitter mis-routes an arm.
- Add an instance-`@service` fixture to `graphitron-sakila-example` as defense-in-depth at the compilation tier. The fixture: a `*Service` class on the example's package with a `(DSLContext ctx)` constructor and one instance method bound by an `@service` directive in the example's SDL (extends an existing query, not a brand-new field, so the addition stays minimal). The compile-tier check at `graphitron-sakila-example` already runs under `mvn install -Plocal-db` per `rewrite-design-principles.adoc:136-140`. The structural test above is the first line of defense; the compile-tier fixture is the type-correctness backstop that catches "the emitted code wouldn't even compile against real jOOQ" — a different signal class than arm routing.

### Phase E — Documentation: anchor the legacy holder shape as the chosen recipe (§5, §7)

§5 of the architect review framed the per-call `new Service(dsl)` emission as a runtime-extension-point question: option (a) accept the legacy shape and document it; option (b) carve out a `ServiceHolderFactory` extension point. The Spec call is **(a)**. Rationale, in order of weight:

1. *Stability through simplicity* (`docs/graphitron-principles.adoc:23`). The legacy shape works in production today across multiple Sikt consumers; introducing a factory adds an extension surface where none was needed.
2. *Schema directives carry business semantics* (`runtime-extension-points.adoc:167-170`). Consumers who need different holder construction can already swap by changing the `@service` class binding in SDL — the indirection is via the schema, not via a runtime hook.
3. *Cross-cutting jOOQ concerns belong on `Configuration`* (`runtime-extension-points.adoc:158-181`). DI and shared state across services compose with the existing `getDslContext` extension point: a holder that needs more than `DSLContext` reads tenant-scoped state from the `DSLContext`'s `Configuration` (or per-request args from `getContextArgument`), not from an injected dependency graph.

So:

- Append one paragraph under `runtime-extension-points.adoc`'s "Complementary Technologies" section (between "Where each concern belongs" and "jOOQ Configuration") titled "Instance `@service` holders": "instance `@service` classes are constructed per call via `new ClassName(DSLContext)`. The holder is created fresh per fetcher invocation; do not stash request-scoped state on instance fields. For per-tenant or per-request decisions inside the holder, ride into the holder via the `DSLContext`'s `Configuration` — `Configuration.data(key)` for ad-hoc per-request values written by your `getDslContext` implementation, `Configuration.settings()` and registered `Converter` / `RecordMapperProvider` for cross-cutting jOOQ behaviour. For SDL-visible per-request arguments, prefer a static method that takes the value via `getContextArgument`. The `(DSLContext)`-only constructor is a deliberate constraint: every per-request seam already exists on `Configuration` or in the SDL." Keep it ~6 sentences; this is documentation of an existing recipe, anchored to existing seams, not a new contract.
- Append one line to `graphitron-rewrite/roadmap/changelog.md` for §7: "R87 (`4867dc0` + this item's landing commit): `@service` directives now classify instance methods on `(DSLContext)` holders, restoring legacy parity. The static/instance fork lives on `MethodRef.CallShape`; emitter dispatches via `serviceCallTarget`. Out-of-band: `ServiceHolderFactory` extension point not added — see runtime-extension-points.adoc."

## Tests

- **Unit tier on `CallShape`.** `MethodRefCallShapeTest` (new): asserts the sealed `Static` / `InstanceWithDslHolder` arms classify under `ServiceCatalog.reflectServiceMethod` for the three populations (`TestServiceStub` static; `TestInstanceServiceStub` instance with ctor; `TestInstanceServiceStubNoCtor` rejected). Replaces the two retired body-string tests. Asserts via `CodeBlock` structural equality on `serviceCallTarget`'s output.
- **Unit tier on `serviceCallTarget`.** A focused test asserts both `CallShape` arms render the expected `CodeBlock` shape, and that the exhaustive switch is closed (a sealed-switch refactor that drops one arm becomes a compile error, not a runtime fall-through).
- **Validator tier.** `ServiceFieldValidationTest.instanceServiceHolderShape_noCtor_validatorReportsAuthorError` (new, Phase C): drives a real schema bound to `TestInstanceServiceStubNoCtor`, asserts the validator emits one `ValidationError` whose message names both fix options.
- **Pipeline tier.** Existing pipeline tests already cover the static-call emission shape; add one pipeline test driving an instance-`@service` field through `GraphitronSchemaBuilder` to a generated `TypeSpec`, asserting on the resulting fetcher method's signature (return type, parameter list) and the `MethodRef.Service.callShape()` arm of the model — *not* on emitted body strings. Lives at `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest` alongside the existing service-fetcher pipeline tests.
- **Compilation tier.** New instance-`@service` fixture in `graphitron-sakila-example` (Phase D): one `*Service` class with a `(DSLContext)` ctor, one instance method bound by an SDL `@service` directive. The existing `mvn install -Plocal-db` flow compiles the generated source against real jOOQ, catching emitter regressions in the form `javac` rejects.
- **Mass migration verified by existing suites.** Phase B's variant split is verified by every test in the `MethodRef.Basic` call-site list above continuing to pass after migration to `TestFixtures.staticOnlyMethodRef(...)` / `TestFixtures.serviceMethodRef(..., CallShape)`. No new assertions land alongside the migration.

## Suggested execution order

Phase A is the architectural core (variant split + `CallShape` lift). Phase B is now load-bearing-paired: the new `service-catalog-tablemethod-must-be-static` producer check ships in the same commit as the variant split that introduces the matching `StaticOnly`-only emitter assumption — splitting the two would leave a window where the assumption is unbacked, which the *Classifier guarantees shape emitter assumptions* discipline rejects. Phase C is a one-test add and can land before, with, or after A; Phase B's analogous validator-tier test for the new `@tableMethod` rejection ships with B by the same rule. Phase D is concrete and cheap; can ride with A/B or in a fresh commit. Phase E is docs-only and lands last so the changelog entry can cite the closing commit SHA.

## Out of scope

- **Carving a `ServiceHolderFactory` runtime extension point (§5 option b).** Rejected per Phase E: the existing `getDslContext` + SDL-level class binding + `Configuration.data(key)` cover the surface; introducing a factory inverts the "stability through simplicity" principle. If a real consumer hits a case where none of those seams suffice, they file it as a fresh roadmap item with the consumer pattern attached.
- **Widening `(DSLContext)` to other holder constructor shapes (no-arg, multi-arg, builder).** The single-`DSLContext`-ctor contract mirrors the legacy generator exactly; widening would need its own design pass and a real consumer asking for it.
- **Adding a sealed permit for every conceptual `MethodRef` shape.** This item lifts the variant population from `{ Basic, ConditionFilter }` to `{ Service, StaticOnly, ConditionFilter }`. Further splits — for example separating `@tableMethod` and `@externalField` despite both projecting to `StaticOnly` — are out of scope; the call-site emit shape is the same for both, so the variant identity is shared.

## Success criteria

- `MethodRef.isStatic()` accessor is gone; `MethodRef` is `sealed permits Service, StaticOnly, ConditionFilter`. The static-vs-instance axis is visible only on `Service.callShape()` — the `StaticOnly` and `ConditionFilter` variants have no `callShape()` accessor at all. `git grep "isStatic"` under `graphitron-rewrite/graphitron/src/main` returns only the `Modifier.isStatic` reflection-API reads in `ServiceCatalog`.
- `MethodRef.Basic` is replaced by two records (`Service`, `StaticOnly`) each with one canonical constructor and no defaults.
- `CallShape.Static(boolean needsDslLocal)` carries the param-derived `needsDsl` pre-resolved at classify time. `serviceCallTarget` takes a narrowed `MethodRef.Service` parameter and switches exhaustively on `CallShape`. `buildServiceFetcherCommon` and `buildServiceRowsMethod` read `needsDsl` from the `CallShape` arm directly; neither site recomputes the param-side disjunction.
- `reflectTableMethod` carries the new `service-catalog-tablemethod-must-be-static` `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck`; the matching `@DependsOnClassifierCheck` is on the `@tableMethod` consumer in `TypeFetcherGenerator`. `LoadBearingGuaranteeAuditTest` passes with both halves of the new pair declared.
- `TypeFetcherGenerator.java:1175-1181` and `:2419-2423`'s `reliesOn` prose no longer duplicates the same paragraph.
- The two body-string tests at `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest.java:1004-1007` and `:1025-1028` are retired; replacement structural-tier `MethodRefCallShapeTest` (asserting via `CodeBlock` equality on `serviceCallTarget`'s output) plus the instance-`@service` fixture in `graphitron-sakila-example` are in place.
- Two new validator-tier tests exercise the instance-holder rejection arm (`ServiceFieldValidationTest`) and the instance-`@tableMethod` rejection arm (`TableMethodFieldValidationTest`) through the validator's own surface.
- `runtime-extension-points.adoc` carries the new "Instance `@service` holders" paragraph (anchored to `Configuration.data(key)` / `Configuration.settings()` / `getContextArgument`); `graphitron-rewrite/roadmap/changelog.md` carries the R87 line.
- `mvn -f graphitron-rewrite/pom.xml install -Plocal-db` passes 10/10 modules, including `graphitron-sakila-example`'s compilation against the new instance-`@service` fixture.

Principles cited: *Narrow component types over broad interfaces*; *Sealed hierarchies over enums for typed information*; *Classifier guarantees shape emitter assumptions* (with the producer-side `@LoadBearingClassifierCheck` landing in the same commit as the new emitter assumption); *Validator mirrors classifier invariants*; *Pipeline tests are the primary behavioural tier*; *Code-string assertions on generated method bodies are banned*; *Stability through simplicity*; *Generation-thinking* (one classification, one carrier, computed once at the parse boundary).
