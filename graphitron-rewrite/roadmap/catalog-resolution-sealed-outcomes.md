# Sealed resolution outcomes for catalog table/FK lookups

R78 lifted the catalog-resolution boundary onto typed values (`TableRef`, `ForeignKeyRef`) but stopped short of giving the *failure* side of those resolutions a typed shape. Three R78-adjacent smells share one root cause and want one fix:

1. **`JoinStep.FkJoin.fk` is a documented-nullable model field.** The javadoc admits two regimes ("null when the jOOQ catalog is not available (unit tests)") and 26 test fixtures construct `new JoinStep.FkJoin(name, null, …)`. Four emit sites — `InlineTableFieldEmitter:122`, `InlineLookupTableFieldEmitter:190`, `SplitRowsMethodEmitter:508` and `:839` — dereference `bridging.fk().keysClass()` with no null-check, relying on a classifier guarantee that is not type-encoded. The redundant `String fkName` component exists *because* `fk` can be null; in the typed-fk case `fk.sqlName()` carries the same data. This is the "tri-state with implicit-only-this-state-reaches-here" anti-pattern that *Builder-step results are sealed, not strings or out-params* names directly.

2. **`Optional<TableEntry>` collapses two meaningfully different outcomes into one.** `JooqCatalog.findTable(String)` returns `Optional.empty()` for both "name not in catalog" and "ambiguous across schemas" (the multi-schema bug R78 fixed). R78 added `findCandidateSchemasFor()` to recover the ambiguity reason; one production caller consumes it (`BuildContext.unknownTableRejection:451` for `@table` directive misses) but the other `findTable`-empty sites listed below do not. The user-facing diagnostic for `@table(name: "event")` ambiguous between `multischema_a` and `multischema_b` ends up being "table 'event' is not in the jOOQ catalog" rather than "qualify with multischema_a.event or multischema_b.event" at every site that isn't `unknownTableRejection`. The fixture's whole point — exposing the ambiguity branch — only reaches the schema author through one path.

3. **The same "not in catalog" string is fabricated at eight call sites with subtle wording drift.** R78 introduced this as a side-effect of #2. Two predicate shapes share one diagnostic intent ("the catalog cannot resolve this name"): `Optional<TableRef>.isEmpty()` at `BuildContext.buildInputNodeIdReference:1006`, the `IdReference` synthesis-shim arm at `:1161`, `NodeIdLeafResolver.resolve:264`, and the DML-payload assembly at `FieldBuilder.resolveDmlPayloadAssembly:1642`; `Optional<FkJoin>.isEmpty()` at `BuildContext.parsePath:541`, `parsePathElement:681` and `:708`, and `NodeIdLeafResolver.resolveFkJoinPath:409` (where `synthesizeFkJoin` returns empty after its internal `findForeignKeyByName`/table lookup misses, conflating two distinct catalog failures: an endpoint table missing vs. the FK name itself missing). All eight sites do `return Rejected("…some variation of 'X is not in the jOOQ catalog'…");`. Wording drifts: "touches a table whose schema package is not generated" / "is not in the jOOQ catalog" / "is not a fully resolved table in the jOOQ catalog". *Generation-thinking*: the same predicate evaluated by multiple consumers means the resolver is under-specified.

The unifying fix is two sealed result types at the catalog boundary (one for tables, one for foreign keys), a non-null `ForeignKeyRef` on `FkJoin`, and a build-time precondition for the degenerate-codegen case that doesn't belong in either resolution sub-taxonomy:

- `TableResolution.{Resolved(TableEntry) | NotInCatalog | Ambiguous(List<String> schemas)}` returned by `findTable(String)`. Classifiers switch exhaustively; the `Ambiguous` arm carries the candidates straight into a single well-written diagnostic. Drops `findCandidateSchemasFor` as redundant. The "schema package has no `Tables` class" case (and adjacent `<records>false</records>` / `<keys>false</keys>` cases) is *not* a fourth arm — those aren't per-lookup outcomes, they're whole-catalog misconfigurations that break every emitter site, so they belong at the catalog-construction boundary as a precondition (see below), not in a sealed result a per-site switch has to handle.
- `ForeignKeyResolution.{Resolved(ForeignKeyRef) | NotInCatalog}` returned by `findForeignKeyByName(String)`. Symmetric in spirit to `TableResolution`, with one failure arm because FK names are scoped to a `Keys` class so cross-schema ambiguity doesn't apply at this lookup. This lets `synthesizeFkJoin` distinguish "endpoint table missing" from "FK name missing" instead of conflating both into `Optional.empty()`.
- `JoinStep.FkJoin.fk` becomes non-null `ForeignKeyRef`. `BuildContext.synthesizeFkJoin` returns a new sealed `FkJoinResolution.{Resolved(FkJoin) | UnknownTable(String requestedName, TableResolution failure) | UnknownForeignKey(String fkName)}` (replacing today's `Optional<FkJoin>`); the four `Optional<FkJoin>` consumer sites switch on it directly. Drop the redundant `String fkName` component on `FkJoin` — `fk.sqlName()` carries it. The current `.orElse(null)` at `BuildContext.java:575` goes away: `findForeignKeyByName`'s `NotInCatalog` arm flows through `synthesizeFkJoin` as `UnknownForeignKey`, and the table lookups at the same site flow as `UnknownTable` carrying the failing `TableResolution` (`NotInCatalog | Ambiguous`) directly. Test fixtures construct synthetic `ForeignKeyRef`s through a new `TestFixtures.foreignKeyRef(...)` factory analogous to R78's `tableRef(...)` factory; the 26 `null`-fk fixture literals across `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest`, `NestingFieldValidationTest`, and the `*FieldValidationTest` family migrate uniformly.
- The eight-site error-string fabrication (four `Optional<TableRef>` sites + four `Optional<FkJoin>` sites; see smell #3) collapses into one diagnostic builder. The four table sites switch on `TableResolution` (3 arms); the four FK sites switch on `FkJoinResolution` (3 arms, two of which delegate back into the same builder by handing it the carried `TableResolution failure` or `String fkName`). Per-site control flow shrinks to a single switch with no string-fabrication branch. The existing `BuildContext.unknownTableRejection` builder is the seed: it already does the ambiguous/missing dispatch by re-querying `findCandidateSchemasFor`; the sealed types let it read straight from the variant.
- Catalog construction (`JooqCatalog`'s constructor / `loadDefaultCatalog`) gains a precondition check: every schema present in the live jOOQ `Catalog` must publish a `<package>.Tables` class, otherwise the build fails fast with a clear "jOOQ codegen produced no `Tables` class for schema 'X' — check `<tables>false</tables>` in your codegen config". This makes R78's defensive `TableEntry.toTableRef`-empty branch unreachable; collapse it to a non-`Optional` return in the same edit. `<records>false</records>` and `<keys>false</keys>` fail by the same family at adjacent reflection sites (`recordClass()`, `Keys` lookup); enumerating those is out of scope for this item — a follow-up roadmap entry can broaden the precondition if a real consumer hits them.

Drive-by while editing this surface: `ServiceDirectiveResolver.computeExpectedServiceReturnType`'s javadoc at `:246` still describes the `TableBoundReturnType + Single` arm as `<jooqPackage>.tables.records.<TableName>Record`. R78 retired the `jooqPackage` threading; the post-R78 phrasing is `<schemaPackage>.tables.records.<TableName>Record`. One-line fix in the same commit that tightens this surface.

## Implementation

**Catalog boundary (`graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/JooqCatalog.java`).**
- Introduce `sealed interface TableResolution permits Resolved, NotInCatalog, Ambiguous` (record arms; `Resolved` carries `TableEntry`, `Ambiguous` carries `List<String> schemas`).
- Introduce `sealed interface ForeignKeyResolution permits Resolved, NotInCatalog` (record arms; `Resolved` carries `ForeignKeyRef`).
- `findTable(String)` returns `TableResolution`. The two-arg `findTable(schema, table)` keeps `Optional<TableEntry>` for now (qualified miss is one-armed; only the unqualified resolver fans out into the sub-taxonomy).
- `findForeignKeyByName(String)` returns `ForeignKeyResolution` instead of `Optional<ForeignKeyRef>`.
- Change `findUnqualifiedTable`'s signature to `private List<TableEntry> findUnqualifiedTable(String unqualifiedSqlName)` and have it return the full candidate list directly (drop the existing `.limit(2)` short-circuit — collisions are expected to be small and `Ambiguous` needs the full list for its diagnostic). `findTable(String)` then forks once: empty → `NotInCatalog`; size 1 → `Resolved`; size ≥ 2 → `Ambiguous(schemaNamesOf(matches))`. Drop the trailing `.size() == 1 ? Optional.of(...) : Optional.empty()` collapse.
- Delete `findCandidateSchemasFor(String)`; the `Ambiguous` variant carries the same data inline.
- `findRecordClass` is a one-liner over `findTable`; rewrite as a switch over `TableResolution` so `FieldBuilder.resolveDmlPayloadAssembly` sees the same variant. Its return remains `Optional<Class<?>>` (the caller already does `.orElse(null)` and folds the empty case into a single rejection); the diagnostic-builder wiring at the call site provides the variant-keyed message, not `findRecordClass` itself.
- Catalog-construction precondition: in the constructor (or `loadDefaultCatalog`), iterate `catalog.schemaStream()` and assert each schema has a `tablesClass(...)` present. On miss, throw `IllegalStateException` with the "no `Tables` class for schema 'X'" message; the Mojo wraps this into a build-boundary diagnostic. After this lands, `TableEntry.toTableRef(String)` returns `TableRef` (not `Optional<TableRef>`); the sole `constantsClass().isEmpty()` arm at `JooqCatalog.java:766-778` becomes unreachable and is removed.

**Diagnostic builder.**
- Promote `BuildContext.unknownTableRejection` to a switch over `TableResolution`: `Ambiguous → structural rejection naming the schemas`; `NotInCatalog → unknownTable rejection with Levenshtein candidates`. The current re-query of `findCandidateSchemasFor` at `BuildContext:451` collapses into a direct field read on the `Ambiguous` variant. Add a sibling `unknownForeignKeyRejection(String fkName)` for the FK-name-miss case (Levenshtein candidates over the union of `Keys` constants in scope).
- The four `Optional<TableRef>` sites (`buildInputNodeIdReference:1006`, `:1161`, `NodeIdLeafResolver:264`, `FieldBuilder:1642`) switch directly on `findTable(name)`. Per-site control flow shrinks to `switch (catalog.findTable(name)) { case Resolved r -> r.tableEntry(); case NotInCatalog n -> return Unresolved(unknownTableRejection(n, name)); case Ambiguous a -> return Unresolved(unknownTableRejection(a, name)); }`.
- The four `Optional<FkJoin>` sites (`parsePath:541`, `parsePathElement:681` and `:708`, `NodeIdLeafResolver:409`) switch on `synthesizeFkJoin`'s new `FkJoinResolution`: `Resolved` continues as today; `UnknownTable(name, failure)` delegates into `unknownTableRejection(failure, name)`; `UnknownForeignKey(fkName)` calls `unknownForeignKeyRejection(fkName)`. No site fabricates a rejection string locally.

**Model (`graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/model/JoinStep.java`).**
- Drop the `String fkName` component of `FkJoin`; tighten `ForeignKeyRef fk` to non-null. Update the record header javadoc at `JoinStep.java:88-92` (the "{@code null} when the jOOQ catalog is not available" sentence goes away). Four emit-site dereferences (`InlineTableFieldEmitter:122`, `InlineLookupTableFieldEmitter:190`, `SplitRowsMethodEmitter:508`, `:839`) keep their existing call shape; what changes is that the type system now guarantees what they already assume.
- `BuildContext.synthesizeFkJoin:575` returns the new `FkJoinResolution` instead of `Optional<FkJoin>`. Its body becomes a switch over `findForeignKeyByName`'s `ForeignKeyResolution` and the endpoint `findTable` calls: a non-`Resolved` table becomes `UnknownTable(name, failure)`; a `NotInCatalog` foreign key becomes `UnknownForeignKey(fkName)`. The two callers at `BuildContext:537` and `:706` switch on `FkJoinResolution` instead of `Optional<FkJoin>`; the existing rejection lambdas there move into the diagnostic builder per the previous section.

**Test fixture migration.**
- New `TestFixtures.foreignKeyRef(String name, String constantName, ClassName keysClass)` factory, mirroring the existing `tableRef(...)` factory at `TestFixtures.java:37`. A short SDL-style helper for the common case (FK name only, synthesise constantName + keysClass from `TEST_JOOQ_ROOT`) keeps the 26 fixture call sites concise.
- Migrate the 26 `new FkJoin(name, null, …)` literals across `TypeFetcherGeneratorTest`, `NestingFieldValidationTest`, `TableFieldValidationTest`, `TableMethodFieldValidationTest`, `SplitTableFieldValidationTest`, `RecordTableFieldValidationTest`, `RecordLookupTableFieldValidationTest`, `ColumnReferenceFieldValidationTest`. Mechanical sweep; no behaviour changes.

**Drive-by.**
- `ServiceDirectiveResolver.java:246`: `<jooqPackage>` → `<schemaPackage>` in the javadoc bullet.

## Tests

- **Unit tier on `TableResolution`.** Extend `JooqCatalogMultiSchemaTest` to assert the three arms by name (today the test asserts via `findCandidateSchemasFor`; that helper is going away). Drive each arm through the multi-schema fixture: `Resolved` for `widget`, `Ambiguous(["multischema_a", "multischema_b"])` for `event`, `NotInCatalog` for `made_up_name`.
- **Unit tier on `ForeignKeyResolution`.** A focused test on `findForeignKeyByName` asserts both arms: `Resolved(ForeignKeyRef)` for a known FK constant, `NotInCatalog` for a fabricated name. Pair with a `synthesizeFkJoin` test that exercises all three `FkJoinResolution` arms — `Resolved`, `UnknownTable` (force one endpoint to a fabricated name), `UnknownForeignKey` (force the FK name to a fabricated value).
- **Catalog-construction precondition.** A unit test asserts that constructing a `JooqCatalog` over a synthetic catalog whose schema package has no `Tables` class throws `IllegalStateException` naming the schema and pointing at the `<tables>` codegen flag. (The synthetic-catalog construction reuses the same fixture-loading shape as `JooqCatalogMultiSchemaTest`.)
- **Diagnostic builder.** A focused unit test on `unknownTableRejection` and `unknownForeignKeyRejection` asserts each variant produces the expected `Rejection` shape — `structural` with the schema list for `Ambiguous`, `unknownTable` with Levenshtein candidates for `NotInCatalog`, `unknownForeignKey` with Levenshtein candidates over `Keys` constants for the FK miss.
- **Pipeline tier.** No new pipeline test in this item: the existing pipeline tests that go through the eight rejection sites already cover the wiring. Once R83 lands its multi-schema pipeline coverage, the `Ambiguous` diagnostic gets end-to-end validation for free.
- **Fixture migration.** The 26-fixture sweep is verified by the suites continuing to pass; no new assertions land alongside the migration.

## Out of scope

- Relaxing `synthesizeFkJoin`'s endpoint resolution further (already `Optional<FkJoin>`).
- The multi-schema fixture itself (R78 shipped it; R83 promotes it to higher tiers).
- The two-arg `findTable(schema, table)` overload — qualified miss is one-armed, no sub-taxonomy needed.
- Broadening the catalog-construction precondition beyond `<tables>false</tables>` to cover `<records>false</records>` and `<keys>false</keys>`. Those break the same family of emitter sites but at adjacent reflection points (`recordClass()`, `Keys` lookup); a follow-up roadmap entry can lift them into the same precondition if a real consumer hits them. This item commits only to the `Tables`-class check because that's the one the new sealed type's `Resolved` arm directly depends on.

Principles cited: *Sub-taxonomies for resolution outcomes*; *Builder-step results are sealed, not strings or out-params*; *Generation-thinking* (rule of thumb: same predicate evaluated by multiple consumers belongs in the model).
