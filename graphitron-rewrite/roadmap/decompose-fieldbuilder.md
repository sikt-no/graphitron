# Decompose `FieldBuilder`

`FieldBuilder.java` is 2,670 lines after the directive-axis pass and the first four projection-resolver lifts: Phase 1 lifted `@service` into [`ServiceDirectiveResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/ServiceDirectiveResolver.java), Phase 2a lifted `@tableMethod` into [`TableMethodDirectiveResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/TableMethodDirectiveResolver.java), Phase 2b lifted `@externalField` into [`ExternalFieldDirectiveResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/ExternalFieldDirectiveResolver.java), Phase 2c lifted `@lookupKey` directive-level invariants into [`LookupKeyDirectiveResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/LookupKeyDirectiveResolver.java), Phase 5 lifted OrderBy projection (~230 lines across five tightly coupled helpers) into [`OrderByResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/OrderByResolver.java), Phase 6a lifted lookup-mapping projection into [`LookupMappingResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/LookupMappingResolver.java), Phase 6b lifted pagination projection (projection + arg-name predicate + default-page-size resolver) into [`PaginationResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/PaginationResolver.java), and Phase 6c lifted `@condition` resolution (arg-level + field-level + nested-rewrap) into [`ConditionResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/ConditionResolver.java). Argument-resolution unification has shipped (Phase 4 landed under Done), so this is no longer blocked.

## The wrong axis

The file is factored along the wrong axis. Today the top-level dispatch is parent-context-first (`classifyField` routes through `classifyRootField` to `classifyQueryField`, `classifyMutationField`, `classifyChildFieldOnResultType`, `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`, `classifyChildFieldOnErrorType`, `classifyObjectReturnChildField`). Each arm then handles every cross-cutting concern itself: it resolves directives, computes orderBy specs, builds condition filters, derives pagination, projects lookup mappings, classifies inputs.

That factoring leaves every cross-cutting concern in one of two suboptimal states:

1. *Resolved inline within each parent-context arm*, producing duplication. The four directives (`@service`, `@tableMethod`, `@externalField`, `@lookupKey`) are in this state.
2. *Factored as a private method of `FieldBuilder`*, avoiding duplication but bundling unrelated concerns into one class with one test surface. OrderBy, pagination, conditions, lookup-mapping projection, input-field classification, mutation-input classification, and conflict detection are in this state.

`ArgumentRef` is the one cross-cutting concern in the file's surroundings that has been factored along the right axis: each argument is classified once into a sealed variant, then projected exhaustively into `GeneratedConditionFilter`, `LookupMapping`, `OrderBySpec`, `PaginationSpec`. Principle 7 ("Builder-internal sealed hierarchies for multi-target classification") in `rewrite-design-principles.adoc` endorses exactly this shape. `FieldBuilder` as a whole is not in it.

## Cross-cutting concerns visible in the file

Each of these concerns is homologous to `ArgumentRef`: a focused input, a sealed result, no genuine dependency on `FieldBuilder` instance state beyond `BuildContext` and `ServiceCatalog`.

### Directive resolution (state 1: inline duplication)

- `@service` resolution + validation + construction in four arms: `classifyQueryField`, `classifyMutationField`, `classifyChildFieldOnResultType`, `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`. Each arm carries its own `resolveServiceField` call, error chain, and `switch (svcResult.returnType())` dispatch with a polymorphic-not-supported `default` rejection. The rejection string is byte-identical across all four arms; a sibling `@record type returning a polymorphic type is not yet supported` rejection in the same shape lives in the result-type arm.
- `@tableMethod` is handled in `classifyQueryField` and `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`; each calls `parseExternalRef` + `svc.reflectTableMethod` with the same shape and similar follow-on validation.
- `@externalField` is handled in `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`; `@lookupKey` is handled in two distinct arms (`classifyQueryField` and the lookup-key path inside `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`).

### Projection concerns (state 2: bundled monolith)

- *OrderBy resolution* — ~230 lines across `resolveOrderByArgSpec`, `resolveDefaultOrderSpec`, `resolveColumnOrderSpec`, `resolveIndexColumns`, `resolveOrderEntries`. **Phase 5 — shipped** (lifted into [`OrderByResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/OrderByResolver.java)).
- *Pagination resolution* (`projectPaginationSpec`, `isPaginationArg`, `resolveDefaultFirstValue`). **Phase 6b — shipped** (lifted into [`PaginationResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/PaginationResolver.java)).
- *Condition resolution* (~200 lines across `buildArgCondition`, `buildFieldCondition`, `rewrapForNested`, plus the enum sub-helpers `buildTextEnumMapping` / `validateEnumFilter`). **Phase 6c — partially shipped** (the three `@condition`-related methods lifted into [`ConditionResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/ConditionResolver.java); the enum sub-helpers stayed in `FieldBuilder` since they serve broader argument-classification needs rather than `@condition` resolution specifically — they fold into a future enum-mapping axis lift in mop-up).
- *Lookup-mapping projection* (`projectForLookup`). **Phase 6a — shipped** (lifted into [`LookupMappingResolver`](../graphitron/src/main/java/no/sikt/graphitron/rewrite/LookupMappingResolver.java)).
- *Input-field classification* (`classifyPlainInputFields`).
- *Mutation-input classification* (~120 lines across `classifyMutationInput`, `validateMutationReturnType`, `getMutationTypeName`).
- *Conflict detection* (`detectQueryFieldConflict`, `detectChildFieldConflict`).

## The pivot

Factor each cross-cutting concern as its own resolver returning a sealed `Resolved<X>`. The classify arms shrink to orchestrators: given a (fieldDef, parentType), call the relevant resolvers and project the resolutions into the appropriate variant.

Likely shape:

*Directive resolvers* (eliminate inline duplication and byte-identical rejection strings):

- `ServiceDirectiveResolver` (`@service`: method lookup, return-type classification, root invariants, errors-channel lift, polymorphic-not-supported rejection). **Phase 1 — shipped.**
- `TableMethodDirectiveResolver` (`@tableMethod`: argMapping, `svc.reflectTableMethod`, expected-return-class strict check, root-only Connection / non-table-bound rejections). **Phase 2a — shipped.**
- `ExternalFieldDirectiveResolver` (`@externalField`: alias-collision check, methodName defaulting, parent-table-class check via `svc.reflectExternalField`). **Phase 2b — shipped.**
- `LookupKeyDirectiveResolver` (`@lookupKey`: directive-level invariants — target-table check at root, byte-identical `@asConnection` cardinality rejection and per-context Single-cardinality rejection at child sites). **Phase 2c — shipped.** Mapping projection (`projectForLookup`) was *not* folded in: it lives inside `resolveTableFieldComponents` (a state-2 monolith) and post-dates argument classification, so it doesn't fit the directive-axis shape; it folds into a dedicated `LookupMappingResolver` under the projection-resolver phase.

The shipped Phase 1 resolver returns a sealed `Resolved` with a three-arm `Success` sub-interface (`TableBound` / `Result` / `Scalar`, each carrying the resolved `MethodRef` and typed `ReturnTypeRef`), an `ErrorsLifted` arm for the polymorphic-of-`@error` lift, and a `Rejected(kind, message)` arm absorbing every error path (parse failure, root-invariants failure, polymorphic-not-supported). Each classify arm projects `Success` into its specific variant (`QueryServiceTableField` / `MutationServiceTableField` / `ServiceTableField`) since variant identity differs across parent contexts. Parent-context-only concerns (join-path parse, the `@record`-typed-parent DEFERRED rejection on result-type parents) stay in the classify arm. Subsequent directive resolvers should follow the same `Resolved`/`Success` shape, keeping per-arm projection out of the resolver.

Phase 2a's `TableMethodDirectiveResolver` follows the same shape with three `Resolved` arms (`TableBound` / `NonTableBound` / `Rejected`), gated by an `isRoot` parameter so the root-only invariants (Connection rejection, non-`TableBound` rejection) fire only at root sites; child sites pass `false` and accept `NonTableBound` returns (today a deferred stub via R43, but the resolver is shape-ready). The root call site asserts `NonTableBound` is unreachable via an `IllegalStateException` arm — sealed exhaustiveness forces the unreachable case to be acknowledged at the call site, which is the idiomatic Java pattern for "this branch is gated upstream." Path-parse for child `@tableMethod` stays in the classify arm (parent-context concern), running before the resolver so a path error surfaces ahead of any reflection failure.

Phase 2b's `ExternalFieldDirectiveResolver` is the simplest of the directive resolvers (single classify site, no `isRoot` axis, no errors-channel lift). It carries a two-arm `Resolved` (`Success(returnType, method)` / `Rejected`) and absorbs the alias-collision check, the external-reference parse + missing-className rejection, and the `svc.reflectExternalField` call against the parent table's Java class. The classify arm shrank from ~45 lines to ~10 lines: it parses the join path (parent-context concern: uses the parent table's name as the join start, and a path error must surface ahead of reflection failure), then switches over `Resolved` to project `Success` into `ComputedField` carrying the parsed path.

Phase 2c's `LookupKeyDirectiveResolver` is structurally lighter than its siblings: `@lookupKey` doesn't reflect an external Java method, so the resolver is purely directive-level invariants. It exposes two methods: `resolveAtRoot(returnType)` validates the target-table invariant (used by `classifyQueryField`) and `resolveAtChild(tableBoundReturnType, withSplitQuery)` validates the cardinality invariants on a `@table`-parent (used by both the `@splitQuery + @lookupKey` and bare `@lookupKey` arms in `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`). The `withSplitQuery` flag specializes the Single-cardinality message so the original byte-identical inline strings remain identical post-lift. Mapping projection (`projectForLookup`) was deliberately *not* folded in — it lives inside `resolveTableFieldComponents` (a state-2 bundled monolith) and post-dates argument classification, so it'll fold into a dedicated `LookupMappingResolver` in the projection-resolver phase. The third site that builds a `@lookupKey`-typed variant (`classifyChildFieldOnResultType` → `RecordLookupTableField`) does no cardinality validation today; tightening that would be a behavior change separate from this lift.

Helpers shared by directive resolvers (`parseExternalRef`, `parseContextArguments`, `buildWrapper`, `enrichArgExtractions`, `liftToErrorsField`, `fieldArgumentNames`) are package-private members of `FieldBuilder` today; the three reflection-bearing resolvers (`@service`, `@tableMethod`, `@externalField`) hold a reference to `FieldBuilder` and call back through them. The still-shared helpers (`parseExternalRef`, `fieldArgumentNames`) become candidates to migrate to a common location (likely `BuildContext`) in the final mop-up phase.

*Projection resolvers* (lift bundled bulk into focused units, each independently testable):

- `OrderByResolver` — **Phase 5 shipped.** Lifted the largest single projection concern (~230 lines across `projectOrderBySpec`, `resolveDefaultOrderSpec`, `resolveColumnOrderSpec`, `resolveEnumValueOrderSpec`, `resolveIndexColumns`, `resolveOrderEntries`, `resolveOrderByArgSpec`) out of `projectForFilter`. Sealed `Resolved` carries `Ok(OrderBySpec spec)` / `Rejected(String message)`; the resolver owns the canonical fallback "could not resolve @defaultOrder columns in table '...'" message that was previously synthesized at the call site. Skips the `RejectionKind` axis since OrderBy errors are uniformly author-fixable and the call site funnels them through `TableFieldComponents.error(...)` rather than constructing an `UnclassifiedField` with a kind. Internal helpers (`resolveColumnOrderSpec`, `resolveOrderEntries`, `resolveIndexColumns`, `resolveEnumValueOrderSpec`) keep nullable returns since they don't carry rejection-message responsibility — only the public surface gets the sealed result.
- `LookupMappingResolver` — **Phase 6a shipped.** Lifted `projectForLookup` (~37 lines) out of `projectForFilter`. Pure ref-walker: the projection has no rejection paths (every input shape produces a `ColumnMapping`, possibly with empty `args()`), so it returns `LookupMapping` directly without the sealed `Resolved` wrapper used by sibling resolvers. The empty-mapping invariant ("@lookupKey declared but no argument resolved to a lookup column") stays at the `projectForFilter` call site since it consults two pieces of caller state (the field-level `@lookupKey` signal via `hasLookupKeyAnywhere` and the accumulating errors list). The resolver carries no `BuildContext` because the projection is pure: it only reads classified `ArgumentRef` variants. Three mapping arms (`ScalarLookupArg` / `DecodedRecord` / `MapInput`) preserved verbatim from the original switch.
- `PaginationResolver` — **Phase 6b shipped.** Lifted three pagination concerns into one resolver: `projectPaginationSpec` (the projection itself), `isPaginationArg` (the four reserved Connection arg names — used during arg classification to route into `PaginationArgRef`), and `resolveDefaultFirstValue` (reads `@asConnection.defaultFirstValue` for per-carrier page size — used by `buildWrapper`). All three are pagination semantics; clustering them into one resolver is honest cohesion. No `BuildContext` field — the resolver is stateless ref-walking + directive-arg reading. Returns `PaginationSpec` (nullable) directly without a sealed `Resolved` wrapper since pagination has no rejection paths (the classifier already validated PaginationArgRef shape, and the `@asConnection` default synthesis is unconditional).
- `ConditionResolver` — **Phase 6c shipped.** Lifted three `@condition`-related methods (`buildArgCondition`, `buildFieldCondition`, `rewrapForNested`) into one resolver. Two sealed result types (`ArgConditionResult` / `FieldConditionResult`) each carry three arms (`None` / `Ok(payload)` / `Rejected(message)`), eliminating the dual-signal pattern (nullable return + errors-list mutation) that both methods previously used. Three call sites updated: `classifyArgument` and `classifyMutationInput` switch on `ArgConditionResult`, `projectForFilter` switches on `FieldConditionResult` and bails directly on `Rejected` (eliminating the prior `(!errors.isEmpty() && fieldCondition == null && fieldDef.hasAppliedDirective(DIR_CONDITION))` re-check). `walkInputFieldConditions`'s five `rewrapForNested` callers route through the resolver instance. The enum sub-helpers (`buildTextEnumMapping`, `validateEnumFilter`) listed in the original plan as part of "condition resolution" stayed in `FieldBuilder` — they serve broader needs (`classifyArgument`, `deriveExtraction`, `buildLookupBindings`, `enrichArgExtractions`) rather than `@condition` resolution specifically, and clustering them here would be dishonest cohesion. They'll fold into a future enum-mapping axis in the mop-up phase.
- `InputFieldResolver`
- `MutationInputResolver`

*Cross-arm validators* (light): conflict detection extracts as `ConflictDetector` if testability justifies it; otherwise stays as static helpers.

After the extraction, `FieldBuilder` is a small coordinator. Each parent-context arm calls a fixed pipeline of resolvers, then projects into the correct variant. Aligning the arms structurally also surfaces shared shape for free: any arm that builds a `TableField`-shaped variant always needs OrderBy + Pagination + Condition resolutions, so that combination can be packaged once. Today the same combination is open-coded across `resolveTableFieldComponents` callers; after the pivot, it's one named pipeline.

## Approach

Land one resolver at a time. Each is its own commit, mergeable independently:

1. *`ServiceDirectiveResolver`* — **shipped.** Lifted `@service` resolution out of all four arms (`classifyQueryField`, `classifyMutationField`, `classifyChildFieldOnResultType`, `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`); each arm now switches over a sealed `Resolved` to project into its specific variant. The shape (helpers stay package-private on `FieldBuilder`, callback into them through a `FieldBuilder` reference, parent-context concerns stay in the arm) is the template the other directive resolvers should follow.
2. *`TableMethodDirectiveResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 2a).** Lifted `@tableMethod` resolution out of both classify sites (`classifyQueryField`, `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`); each site shrank from ~45 lines to ~10. Sealed `Resolved` carries `TableBound` / `NonTableBound` / `Rejected`, gated by an `isRoot` flag so root-only invariants fire only at root.
3. *`ExternalFieldDirectiveResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 2b).** Lifted `@externalField` resolution out of `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`; the inline block shrank from ~45 lines to ~10. Sealed `Resolved` carries `Success(returnType, method)` / `Rejected`. Absorbs the alias-collision check, external-ref parse, missing-className rejection, and `svc.reflectExternalField`; path-parse stays in the classify arm (it's a parent-context concern and must run ahead of any reflection failure).
4. *`LookupKeyDirectiveResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 2c).** Lifted directive-level `@lookupKey` invariants out of three classify sites (`classifyQueryField`, two arms in `classifyChildFieldOnTableType`). Sealed `Resolved` carries `Ok(returnType)` / `Rejected`; two entry points (`resolveAtRoot`, `resolveAtChild(_, withSplitQuery)`) match the two parent contexts. Eliminates the byte-identical `@asConnection` rejection prose duplicated across the two child arms and centralizes the target-table invariant. Mapping projection (`projectForLookup`) deferred to the projection-resolver phase since it post-dates arg classification.
5. *`OrderByResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 5).** Lifted the largest single projection concern out of `projectForFilter`; the call site shrank from ~7 lines (with dual-signal null + errors-list logic) to ~5 lines (instanceof Rejected + cast Ok). The seven OrderBy methods are gone from `FieldBuilder` (-237 lines); `OrderByResolver` adds 326 lines including comprehensive class-level and method-level Javadoc. Sealed `Resolved` carries `Ok(OrderBySpec)` / `Rejected(String message)`; the resolver absorbs the canonical fallback message previously synthesized at the call site, eliminating the prior dual-signal pattern (orderBy=null + errors-empty meaning "no inner error, here's the fallback").
6. *`LookupMappingResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 6a).** Lifted `projectForLookup` (~37 lines) out of `projectForFilter`; the call site shrank from a private-method call to a one-liner against the resolver instance. Pure projection — no `Resolved` wrapper since the function is total (every classified ref shape produces a `ColumnMapping`, possibly with an empty `args()` list). The empty-mapping invariant remains at the call site because it consults `hasLookupKeyAnywhere(fieldDef)` and the accumulating `errors` list — both caller-side state. The resolver carries no `BuildContext` because the projection is pure ref-walking.
7. *`PaginationResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 6b).** Lifted all three pagination helpers (`projectPaginationSpec`, `isPaginationArg`, `resolveDefaultFirstValue`) from `FieldBuilder` into one resolver. Three call sites updated: `buildWrapper` uses `resolveDefaultFirstValue`, `classifyArgument` uses `isPaginationArg`, `projectForFilter` uses the main `resolve` projection. Stateless instance (no `BuildContext`), no `Resolved` wrapper since pagination has no rejection paths. Removed the now-unused `ARG_DEFAULT_FIRST_VALUE` static import from `FieldBuilder`.
8. *`ConditionResolver`* — **shipped (Phase 6c).** Lifted the three `@condition`-related methods (`buildArgCondition`, `buildFieldCondition`, `rewrapForNested`) out of `FieldBuilder` into a focused resolver. Two sealed result types (`ArgConditionResult` / `FieldConditionResult`), each three-armed (`None` / `Ok` / `Rejected`), replace the prior dual-signal pattern (nullable return + errors-list mutation). Three call sites switch on the new types: `classifyArgument`, `classifyMutationInput` (both via `resolveArg`), and `projectForFilter` (via `resolveField`). The five `rewrapForNested` callers in `walkInputFieldConditions` route through the resolver instance. The enum sub-helpers listed in the plan as part of "condition resolution" were deliberately left in `FieldBuilder` — they're argument-classification utilities, not `@condition`-specific.
9. The remaining projection resolvers (`InputField`, `MutationInput`).
10. Final mop-up: helpers move with their nearest resolver, conflict detection extracts if the testability win justifies it.

## Out of scope

The earlier `QueryFieldBuilder` / `MutationFieldBuilder` / `ChildFieldBuilder` file-split idea is dropped. With every cross-cutting concern lifted into its own file, the remaining classify arms are short and genuinely context-specific; a parent-context-axis split would just shuffle small arms across files. If size remains painful afterwards, file a fresh Backlog item with fresh evidence.

Phase 2a dropped `FieldBuilder` from 3,163 → 3,103 lines (-60) and added 146 lines in `TableMethodDirectiveResolver`; the two `@tableMethod` blocks went from ~45 lines to ~10 lines each. Phase 2c dropped `FieldBuilder` from 3,077 → 3,071 lines (-6) and added 100 lines in `LookupKeyDirectiveResolver`; the line-count delta is small because the two child-site arms each only carried ~10 lines of cardinality rejection prose, but the win is qualitative because the byte-identical `@asConnection` rejection (10 lines duplicated verbatim) is centralized, and the `@lookupKey` directive-level invariants now live in one place. Phase 5 dropped `FieldBuilder` from 3,071 → 2,834 lines (-237) and added 326 lines in `OrderByResolver`; the seven OrderBy methods (entry point + six helpers) moved out wholesale, the call site within `projectForFilter` shrank from a ~7-line null-check + dual-fallback to a 5-line instanceof + Ok-cast, and 9 imports (4 graphql AST/schema types, 5 BuildContext static-imports) became unused and were removed. Phase 6a dropped `FieldBuilder` from 2,834 → 2,787 lines (-47) and added 88 lines in `LookupMappingResolver`; the lift is small in raw line count because `projectForLookup` was already a single tight switch, but the win is conceptual: the projection is now an isolated unit (no `BuildContext`, no errors list), separating the pure ref-walking logic from the call-site empty-mapping validation that consults caller state. Phase 6b dropped `FieldBuilder` from 2,787 → 2,743 lines (-44) and added 109 lines in `PaginationResolver`; three call sites (`buildWrapper`, `classifyArgument`, `projectForFilter`) routed through the new resolver, the `ARG_DEFAULT_FIRST_VALUE` static import became unused and was removed, and the three pagination helpers (`projectPaginationSpec`, `isPaginationArg`, `resolveDefaultFirstValue`) moved out wholesale. Phase 6c dropped `FieldBuilder` from 2,743 → 2,670 lines (-73) and added 176 lines in `ConditionResolver`; three `@condition` methods moved out, three call sites updated to switch on the new sealed result types, and one piece of dual-signal pattern at the `projectForFilter` call site (`!errors.isEmpty() && fieldCondition == null && fieldDef.hasAppliedDirective(DIR_CONDITION)`) collapsed into a direct early-return on `FieldConditionResult.Rejected`. Cumulative: `FieldBuilder` is down 631 lines (3,301 → 2,670, -19.1%) over R6.
